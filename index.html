A Few of My Favorite Campgrounds

by Anne Stahl

Introduction

Over the years, my family and I have frequently enjoyed camping together. Both my husband Edward and I are teachers, so camping has provided us with a budget-friendly way to get out and see the U.S. with our children, Tommy and Heidi. On this site you will see a few of my favorite campgrounds that we have visited. Each of these places has given our family many unique experiences and cherished memories together.

Colter Bay Campground

Location: Grand Teton National Park in Moran, Wyoming

We first experienced Colter Bay in 2018 when we took our three-week camping trip from Missouri to Mendicino, California. Colter Bay is a rugged campground nestled in bear country. As long as you keep your food and toiletries secure in the site’s bear box, you shouldn’t have any problems. That first trip to Colter Bay we didn’t see any bears. However, that wasn’t the case when we returned in 2020…that’s a story for another day. Wink. Wink. The best part about Colter Bay is that it sits right on Jackson Lake against the breathtaking backdrop of The Grand Tetons mountain range. There are serene lakeside beaches that you can easily walk to from your campsite and many rigorous hiking trails that take you into the foothills. Boating on Lake Jackson is also a great time!

D.L. Bliss State Park Campground

Location: Lake Tahoe, California

D.L. Bliss gave us our first taste of California as a family during our 2018 trip. After driving through Nevada, we couldn’t believe the difference in scenery and landscape. The campground itself is tiered up against a very steep rocky wooded hill. Huge boulders were scattered all throughout. The kids loved this! And the pine cones were gigantic! Redwood pine cones, of course. The state park also had a lovely sandy beach on Lake Tahoe which you could access via a ridgetop trail overlooking the lake. I would love to camp there again.

Summerdale Campground

Location: Sierra National Forest in Fish Camp, California

We used Summerdale as our base camp for Yosemite National Park (2018 again). Just south of the park’s entrance, we were able to get an early start into Yosemite before all the RVs jammed up the highway. The campground also has a shimmery stream rambling through. If you follow it up the mountain side a bit you reach an elevated swimming hole where the water cascades over gigantic boulders. The swimming hole has sparkling yellow flecks of fools gold swirling all around. The kids loved swimming there…..until I saw panther scat, then it was time to go. The kids told me later on that I "overreacted".

Betsie River Campsite

Location: Frankfort, Michigan

Bestsie River campground is a very special place to us. This is where we have camped the most with Tommy and Heidi starting back in 2015. In fact, week-long Betsie River camping trips served as training missions for the kids before we set out on our 2018 California trip. We have continued to return to Betsie River any summer that time allows. All year we dream about the sandy Lake Michigan beach and Frankfort’s lake town vibe, with its quaint shops and good food. The campground itself provides a fantastic breakfast in the camp store, where you can also purchase local Michigan ice cream and chocolate milk.

Hawn State Park

Location: St. Genevieve, Missouri

A couple of years ago we discovered Hawn State Park. We wanted to do something over spring break, but we needed to stay closer to home because of Tommy's track schedule. Hawn’s setting provides spacious sites in a vast forest of protected land. (A school teacher named Helen Hawn donated this land to the state in 1952). We took advantage of the hiking trails and enjoyed a peaceful stay in the woods next to the gurgling sounds of Pickle Creek. We left more relaxed and happy than when we arrived.

Onondaga Cave State Park

Location: Leasburg, Missouri

As our kids have gotten older we have had to make sure the camping trips include interesting things to do. Onondaga is perfect for that. Onondaga Campground sits alongside the Meramec River with a nice size gravel bar for playing around in the water. The entrance to Onondaga Cave is just a short walk from the campground. The campground provides tours of the cave, which I have now visited 3 or 4 times. It is always stunning. My kids also enjoy float trips quite a bit. Just down the road about a half mile from the campground is a float trip outfitter called Ozark Outdoors. Not only can they set you up for a float, but they also have a multi-tiered aerial ropes course that even challenged my teenage son and his friends.

Closing

All these campgrounds hold a special place in my heart. Now that my children are older teenagers (19 & 16), camping together has become a rare treat. So, as their mother, the many campgrounds we have visited now symbolize dear memories of our time together. I hope you enjoyed hearing about these places. Maybe you will find yourself at one of them some day, ready to make memories of your own with the people you love.
